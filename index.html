About

Meet Jesse

I'm a photographer with a deep pull toward the outdoors — mountain tops most of all — always chasing interesting, inspiring subjects for my camera and drone.

My favourite things to photograph are landscapes, nature and the Milky Way. I thrive on exploring new places and sharing their beauty so others can enjoy them too.

I walked the Via Alpina — a 2,000 km (~1,200 mile) trail across the Alps from Trieste to Monaco — raising money for men's mental health along the way. Wherever the trail led, the camera came too.

Why I walked

The Via Alpina traces the Alps from the edge of the Adriatic to the shores of the Mediterranean. I walked it end to end — roughly 2,000 kilometres from Trieste to Monaco — with a camera and drone in the pack the whole way.

I did it to raise money for men's mental health and suicide prevention as part of Movember. Every print sold during the journey was donated in full — and the donation page is still open if you'd like to give.
